Building a Powerful, User Friendly Database

Research for Rosenworx was ongoing, as was development. New modules were decided upon and development moved very quickly to respond to the company’s growing and changing needs. When the need for new development arose, meetings would be held with the teams, lead by myself and sometimes joined by the Vice President of Operations. The supervisor(s) of the team(s) we were meeting with would also be present. Users from all departments found that they could access information faster than ever before. Each department had the ability to enter the data that they were responsible for, easily and securely, and data could be viewed by whomever needed it, once again, easily and securely. The production and marketing teams enjoyed some of the greatest benefits of the system. Rosenworx was met with rave reviews throughout the company, for its ability to cut user’s work loads, and simplify and make processes more efficient. My Role: For this project, I performed UX research, ideation, design, development and database administration.

Problem Statement

Rosen Publishing’s scheduling software had an expiration date and was about to be no longer functional. The company needed a solution fast. At the same time, the existing process of constructing catalogs was completely backwards, creating the catalog first, then entering that data into the database. This practice was extremely time consuming and inefficient.

Main Features

  • Clear Data Structure
  • Easily Searchable
  • Easy and Secure Data Entry and Editing Across Departments
  • Scheduling Module
  • Catalog Pagination Module
